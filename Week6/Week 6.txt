Fine-Tuning is Used in Spotify
Spotify uses fine-tuning because a general-purpose LLM does not naturally understand Spotify's specific data, brand voice, and personalization requirements.
Problem Without Fine-Tuning
Suppose Spotify has the following user data:
Top Artist: Taylor Swift
Top Genre: Pop
Listening Time: 25,000 minutes
Most Played Song: Cruel Summer
A normal LLM might generate:
"You listened to a lot of music this year."
This is generic and does not sound like Spotify Wrapped.
Spotify wants something like:
"You spent 25,000 minutes vibing to pop music, with Taylor Swift leading your soundtrack throughout the year."
To achieve this style consistently for millions of users, Spotify fine-tunes the model.
Why Spotify Needed Fine-Tuning
1. Consistent Brand Voice
Spotify wants every Wrapped story to have:
* Fun tone
* Friendly language
* Youthful style
* Personalized storytelling
Fine-tuning teaches the model:
User Listening Data
       ↓
Spotify Style Story


instead of generic text.




2. Better Personalization
The model learns patterns such as:
Listening Behavior
      ↓
Story Pattern


Example:
Rock listener
      ↓
"Your year was powered by guitar riffs."


Classical listener
      ↓
"You spent the year surrounded by timeless masterpieces."


3. Fewer Hallucinations
Without fine-tuning:
User listened 1000 minutes
Model says 5000 minutes


This is a hallucination.
Fine-tuning teaches the model to correctly interpret Spotify's structured listening data.
Where Fine-Tuning is Used in Spotify
Spotify Wrapped
This is the biggest use case.
Input:
Top Artists
Top Songs
Listening Minutes
Genres
Listening Trends
Output:
Personalized Wrapped Story
Example:
25,000 listening minutes
Taylor Swift #1 artist
Pop genre #1
becomes
"Your year belonged to Taylor Swift, who soundtracked most of your 25,000 listening minutes."
AI-Generated Music Insights
Spotify can generate:
* Listening summaries
* Music personality descriptions
* Yearly highlights
using fine-tuned models.


Personalized User Narratives
Instead of showing only numbers:
120 Artists
2000 Songs


the model converts them into a story:
"You explored 120 artists this year, proving your playlist never stayed in one place for long."






























Fine-Tuning in Pinterest
Pinterest uses fine-tuning primarily to improve search relevance and help users discover the most relevant Pins for their queries.
Why Pinterest Needed Fine-Tuning
Pinterest contains billions of Pins with:
* Images
* Titles
* Descriptions
* Board names
* Website links
When a user searches:
modern bedroom ideas
Pinterest must determine which Pins are actually relevant.
A general LLM may understand language but does not know:
* Pinterest-specific content
* User search behavior
* What Pinterest considers a relevant result
Therefore, Pinterest fine-tunes the model on Pinterest data.
Where Fine-Tuning is Used
1. Search Ranking
When a user searches:
minimalist living room
Pinterest needs to rank millions of Pins.
Input:
Query:
"minimalist living room"


Pin:
Title: Modern Living Room Design
Description: Scandinavian interior ideas
Image: Living room photo
Output:
Relevance Score = 0.95
Higher score = shown higher in search results.
2. Recommendation Systems
Pinterest also recommends Pins such as:
You may also like:
- Home decor ideas
- Bedroom inspiration
- Modern furniture
Fine-tuned models help understand the relationship between:
* User interests
* Search history
* Saved Pins
* Pin content
3. Semantic Understanding
Traditional search:
Query: couch ideas
may only look for the word "couch".
Fine-tuned LLM:
couch ≈ sofa
living room furniture
modern seating
It understands meaning, not just keywords.
How Fine-Tuning Works in Pinterest
Step 1: Collect Training Data
Pinterest gathers examples:
Query: modern kitchen
Pin: Kitchen design image
Label: Relevant
Query: modern kitchen
Pin: Dog picture
Label: Not Relevant
Thousands or millions of such examples are collected.
Step 2: Train/Fine-Tune the Model
The model learns:
(Query + Pin Content)
         ↓
Relevance Score
Example:
Query:
"small apartment decor"


Pin:
"Space-saving apartment ideas"


Output:
0.98
Step 3: Learn Pinterest-Specific Patterns
The model learns that users searching for:
boho bedroom
usually prefer:
* Interior design Pins
* Decoration Pins
* Furniture inspiration
instead of unrelated content.
Step 4: Deploy for Real-Time Search
When a user searches:
User Query
     ↓
Fine-Tuned LLM
     ↓
Relevance Score
     ↓
Ranking System
     ↓
Top Pinterest Results




Example
Before Fine-Tuning
Search:
modern office setup
Results may contain:
✓ Office desk
✓ Workspace design


After Fine-Tuning
Search:
modern office setup
Results”
✓ Workspace design
✓ Home office inspiration
✓ Desk organization ideas
✓ Productivity setup
